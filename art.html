Feb.24.2020

I think I was like, 6 and I really liked the Mario vs Donkey Kong game on GBA. Never beat it. Not even today. probably should get around doing that. Did you know that was developed by NST? They're a developer branch for Nintendo over in Redmond, Washington. Really funny to think they're a kinda long drive from me, but not too far either.

Toy collectibles aren't my mom's type of thing, but at least my Dad would occasionally get me actual games and Beyblades. Asides those, I was left with playing with copy paper with my dad. Lots of airplanes and boats were made back then, but I never got too into origami. The commercial for Paper Mario TTYD on GCN made me drop my jaw thanks to it. It's still my most favorite Mario game, albeit it's got some serious issues.

Yeah, Mario and Zelda stuff was that big big *big* big big personal thing. Self-proclaimed religion, like a lot of kids like myself. Mario here, Mario there. I drew Mario all the time and even today I still really suck at drawing him without reference. Toon Link, on the other hand? Hand me the pen, man.

So those Mini Mario's from Mario vs DK? Paper Mario? Hardly having toys asides Beyblades? I folded a sheet of copy paper four-folds (hamburger style) and drew a Mini Mario on both sides. Laminate it with scotch tape to make it near-waterproof. Boom, that was my Mini Mario toy collection as a kid. Like 8 of them during Fun Friday's at school. Teachers thought it was funny, I got distracted in class a lot but never ever ever did I bring my GBA to class. I'm too good of a kid. Speaking of which, for show-and-tell on my birthday during the 1st grade, which was the very last day of school, I brought it and it got stolen. The teacher brought me to the kid who stole it and he was crying and all I could only feel bad for him. I thought he found it and was crying because it wasn't truly his, but then I learned the whole story in the 7th grade when my mom talked about it during a vacation trip.

3rd grade was the cool, everyone was so nice and cool. 4th grade sucked and got depressing, but 5th grade made a runback. I made a Mario fan-comic or something with a best friend named Josh. I kinda just stole jokes from Newgrounds and told them through comics shared among classmates. Not Mario-related, but I also got dared to draw Link with a giant dick and ended up crying when my teacher confronted me about it. My mom still talks about it to this day to make me feel bad. Never works. I swear.

6th grade was a huge transition. No friends because student-sorting between schools sucked and I was alone. Very horrible year when I got all my consoles taken away for getting 3 C's during first quarter. Motivation to draw Mario fan-comics died because of school bullying. I'll save that whole story for another time, don't worry. I got into Pokemon around here, but ended up never getting back into drawing because of the bullying bullshit. Minecraft and library computer time was my high priority.

Scooby Doo's French Class: Part Two. My soon-to-be-retiring French teacher caught me watching anime before class started. I think it was Cromartie High and I remember loving it still. We talked about animation in Europe, and it was crazy for me to look through. One thing led to another and soon enough I was getting active on Newgrounds again, just like what happened back in Elementary. But this time, I was in other places like Twitter and Tumblr. Fanart's amazing, fuck you. More importantly, original art got spicy. Furthermore, indie-games were super cool. Unfortunately, super trash games exist but that's ok. It was all super cool

Wish I could do that. Would be so cool if it was easy. Shut up Marcus

I got a tablet off Amazon, used. Fuck it, I never touched digital art, but it was so cool. I couldn't resist. Did I mention Animal Crossing was another super-big Nintendo game, like Mario/Zelda/Pokemon? Well now you know, like literally everyone else who knew me in high school. Ankha was everywhere. My true love was my first art piece. It sucked, but I needed her. Everyday, in the side of my Geometry book she was there. I was amorous for her. The queen was my light. I need you Ankha.

Self-awareness comes in many forms, many times. The first one was the plainly-obvious love for Gaming(TM). Fanart of my own all over my notebook. Undertale was pretty cool and fresh at the time, but I fell out of it. I forget what "kind of artist I really was" at that point, but I loved fighting games, the secondhand category that rivaled my love for babby Nintendo. Stupid memes and Morrigans and Cozy fighting game characters were essentially my "weird hobby." I grew up eventually, or at least to the point I have to seriously think of a career pathway

So yeah, that's the story so far of how I've become an artist.
